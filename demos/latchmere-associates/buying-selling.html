What we take care of for you

Property transactions involve several moving parts. We handle the legal side so you do not have to.

Searches and checks

Before you commit, we run searches to check for issues with the property — drainage, planning, local authority records. We explain what the results mean in plain English.

Contracts and paperwork

We review the contract, raise questions with the other side, and check the terms before anything is signed. We will not let you exchange until you are fully clear on what you are agreeing to.

Completion and transfer

On completion day, we handle the transfer of funds and register the property in your name. We confirm when everything is done and what happens next.

How we help you through it

A clear path, at your pace. We adapt to your situation and keep you in the picture throughout.

  1. Step 1

    You tell us what you are doing

    Buying, selling, or both — tell us where things are. We take on your case, confirm what we need from you, and explain what happens next before we do anything.

  2. Step 2

    We review the draft contract

    When the seller's solicitor sends the draft contract, we go through it in detail. We raise any questions and flag anything you need to know before you decide to proceed.

  3. Step 3

    We carry out the searches

    We request the legal searches needed to check the property. When results come back, we explain what they show and whether anything needs to be looked into further.

  4. Step 4

    We prepare you to exchange contracts

    Before you exchange, we make sure you understand what you are committing to. Once contracts are exchanged, the sale is legally binding. We will not let you reach this point until you are ready.

  5. Step 5

    We manage completion day

    On completion day, we handle the transfer of funds and confirm when keys can be collected. We deal with the other solicitor directly. You just need to be ready to move.

  6. Step 6

    We register your ownership

    After completion, we register the property in your name at HM Land Registry and send you confirmation once it is done. The file is then closed and we let you know.

Common worries

Things people ask us before they get in touch.

What is a solicitor actually doing during a property purchase?

Quite a lot that is not visible. We check the legal title of the property, run searches, review the contract and raise questions with the seller's solicitor, liaise with your lender, check the transfer deed, and manage the movement of funds on completion day. We then register you as the new owner with HM Land Registry. If anything is not in order, we deal with it before it becomes your problem.

How long does it take?

A straightforward purchase typically takes 8 to 12 weeks from offer accepted to completion. Chains take longer because they depend on everyone being ready at the same time. We will give you an honest estimate based on your situation and keep you updated if anything changes.

What happens if the sale falls through?

Until contracts are exchanged, either side can withdraw. It is frustrating, but it does happen. If it does, we will explain your position clearly, tell you what costs may be involved, and help you decide what to do next. We do not disappear when things get difficult.

Do I need a solicitor if I am not using a mortgage?

You are not legally required to use a solicitor if you are a cash buyer. However, the legal checks — title searches, contract review, Land Registry registration — still need to be done correctly. Most people find that having a solicitor handle this protects them from problems that are hard to spot without legal training.

Can I afford a solicitor?

We offer a free initial conversation so you understand the process and costs before you commit. For most standard purchases and sales, we offer a fixed fee so you know the full amount upfront. We will always tell you what is included and what is not before we begin.

What is Stamp Duty and do I have to deal with it?

Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) is a government tax paid when you buy a property above a certain price. We calculate what you owe, tell you the amount before completion, and submit the return to HMRC on your behalf. You do not have to work this out yourself.
