What happens in a divorce?

A simple overview of the main stages. Every situation is different — we will explain how this applies to you.

  1. Stage 1

    You apply for a divorce

    One or both of you submits an application to the court. Since 2022 this is a no-fault process in England and Wales — you do not need to prove anyone did anything wrong.

  2. Stage 2

    A 20-week waiting period begins

    The law builds in a pause. This gives both people time to consider and to begin sorting out finances and arrangements for any children.

  3. Stage 3

    You apply for a conditional order

    The court checks the paperwork and confirms in principle that you can divorce. Both of you are notified.

  4. Stage 4

    Another 6-week pause

    After the conditional order there is a further waiting period before the divorce can be finalised.

  5. Stage 5

    The final order is granted

    You apply for the final order. Once the court grants it, the marriage is legally ended. Financial agreements made separately are then enforceable.

Why this is here Divorce is the most emotionally charged service on the site. People often do not know how the process works and imagine it to be more adversarial or chaotic than it is. Showing a clear, calm process with plain-English stages reduces anxiety and builds confidence that the firm knows what it is doing. It also models how the firm communicates — no jargon, no drama.
