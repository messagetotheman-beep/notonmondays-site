Tone principles

  • ✓ Use plain English. Write like you are talking to someone who is stressed and in a hurry.
  • ✓ Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
  • ✓ Say "you" and "we". Not "the client" or "the firm".
  • ✓ Give control to the user. Use "you choose", "you decide", "your situation".
  • ✓ Name the feeling before the solution. "Life feels complicated" before "we can help".
  • ✗ No em dashes. Use a comma or rewrite the sentence.
  • ✗ No legal jargon without a plain-English explanation alongside it.
  • ✗ No urgency language ("act now", "don't delay"). These people are already stressed.
  • ✗ No "thought leadership" phrases ("holistic", "bespoke", "tailored solutions").
  • ✗ No passive voice where active is possible.
