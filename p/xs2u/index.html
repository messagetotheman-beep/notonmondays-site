XS2YOU — Website Review and Quick Plan to Go Live

A few simple changes to help you build trust, take payments, and start selling

Not On Mondays helps small businesses get digital work live without the usual complexity. We focus on what actually needs to happen to launch, not what could happen eventually. This proposal is about getting XS2YOU ready to take real orders.

What we're seeing

The site has the right foundations but a few things are holding it back:

  • It looks like a template that hasn't been finished yet
  • Placeholder text and missing information reduce trust
  • It's not clear how to buy or what the checkout process looks like
  • People won't enter card details if they're not sure the site is real
  • The path from browsing to buying feels unclear

Why this matters

People make snap judgements about websites in just a few seconds. If something feels unfinished or unclear, they leave.

We know from years of data that around 70% of people abandon their shopping carts. The main reasons are simple: they don't trust the site, they can't find what they need, or the buying process feels too complicated.

People scan pages rather than read them. They look for quick signals that tell them "this is a real shop" and "I can buy safely here". If those signals are missing, they won't take the risk.

The good news is that these problems are fixable. You don't need to rebuild everything. You just need to address the trust gaps and make the journey clearer.

How we should get XS2YOU live

We should not build anything custom right now. The priority is to get you live quickly and safely.

The best approach is to use Shopify as your ecommerce platform. Shopify handles products, checkout, payments, and security. It's fast to set up, lower risk, and easier for you to manage once it's live.

This means:

  • You can focus on running your business, not managing technical infrastructure
  • Checkout and payments work properly from day one
  • PCI compliance and security are handled for you
  • You can add products, update stock, and manage orders yourself
  • We can get you live in days, not months

The priority is simple: make the site feel real and trustworthy, load real products properly, make checkout secure, and launch quickly.

The 4 things to fix

Fix 1: Make the site feel real and trustworthy

Right now, the site has placeholder content and missing details. This makes people unsure if you're a real business.

What to do:

  • Replace any "lorem ipsum" or template copy with your actual words
  • Add real contact details (email, phone if you have one, business address)
  • Create clear delivery and returns information
  • Add a simple "About" section that tells your story
  • Include trust signals like secure payment badges
Outcome: Customers feel safe buying from you

Fix 2: Make it obvious what you sell

Visitors should understand what you offer within seconds of landing on the homepage.

What to do:

  • Clear categories in your navigation: Women, Men, Accessories
  • Remove vague labels or marketing speak
  • Simplify your homepage message so it says exactly what you do
  • Show your best products or categories immediately
Outcome: Customers understand the site in seconds

Fix 3: Set up products properly in Shopify

Products need to be structured clearly so customers can find what they need and make decisions quickly.

What to do:

  • Set up collections in Shopify (Women, Men, Accessories)
  • Add products with clear images, descriptions, and pricing
  • Include size information and stock status
  • Set delivery timeframes and shipping rules
  • Make sure product pages answer customer questions
Outcome: Customers can browse and choose products easily

Fix 4: Get payments and checkout working in Shopify

This is the most important part. Shopify handles checkout, payments, and security so customers can buy with confidence.

What to do:

  • Set up Shopify Payments (or Stripe)
  • Enable guest checkout so people don't have to create an account
  • Configure shipping rates and delivery options
  • Test the entire checkout flow with a real card
  • Make sure confirmation emails work properly
Outcome: You can start taking real orders safely

What this looks like in practice

Phase 1: Clean up content and structure

  • Replace placeholder copy with real content
  • Add contact details, delivery info, and returns policy
  • Simplify navigation and messaging
  • Create trust signals (About page, security badges)

Phase 2: Set up Shopify properly

  • Configure Shopify account and settings
  • Create product collections (Women, Men, Accessories)
  • Add products with images, descriptions, and pricing
  • Set up Shopify Payments and shipping rules
  • Configure checkout and test the flow

Phase 3: Launch and test

  • Test checkout end to end with real payment
  • Check mobile experience
  • Verify confirmation emails work
  • Go live

Phase 4 (optional): Growth

  • Content creation and social proof
  • Paid ads if needed
  • Email marketing
  • Conversion optimization

This phase happens after launch, once the store is working and you have orders coming in.
