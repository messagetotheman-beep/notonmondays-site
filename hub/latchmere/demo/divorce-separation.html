What you will need to sort out

Most separations involve three areas. You do not have to tackle all of them at once.

👨‍👩‍👧

Your children

Where they live, when they see each parent, who makes day-to-day decisions. Most parents reach an arrangement between themselves, with our help. You do not need a court order if you can agree.

🏠

Your finances

The family home, savings, pensions, and any shared debts. We will help you understand what you are each entitled to and what a fair outcome looks like — without pressure or drama.

📋

Ending the marriage

The legal process to formally end your marriage or civil partnership. Since 2022, neither of you needs to prove the other did anything wrong. We guide you through the paperwork from start to finish.

Why this is here People often arrive thinking "divorce" means one thing. Separating it into three distinct areas reduces the sense of overwhelm and makes it easier to understand what actually needs to be decided. Naming them in human terms ("your children", "your finances") rather than legal categories ("residence arrangements", "financial settlement") keeps it accessible for people with limited legal literacy or English as a second language.

  3. Step 3

    We help you decide what to sort out first

  4. Step 4

    We guide you through the paperwork

    We handle the legal side and explain each step as we go. If your partner has a solicitor, we communicate with them on your behalf. You do not have to deal with that directly.

  5. Step 5

    We help you reach an agreement

    On finances and children, wherever possible we help you agree things without going to court. We can also represent you in mediation if that is the right route.

  6. Step 6

    We see it through to the end with you

    Once everything is agreed and the final order is granted, the marriage is legally ended. We are with you the whole way — and we will tell you clearly when we have reached that point.

Common worries

Things people ask us before they get in touch.

Do I need to prove my partner did something wrong?

No. Since April 2022, divorce in England and Wales is a no-fault process. You do not need to blame anyone or provide reasons. You simply state that the marriage has broken down. This applies to married couples and civil partners.

Will I have to go to court?

Most people do not. The divorce process itself is paperwork — there is no hearing you need to attend. Arrangements for children and finances are also usually agreed without a court appearance. We only go to court if an agreement cannot be reached, and we will always tell you honestly if that looks likely.

What will happen to my children?

Arrangements for your children are separate from the divorce itself. Most parents agree where children live and how they spend time with each parent without needing a court order. We help you think through what works for your family and, if needed, reach a written agreement. The children's wellbeing is always the starting point.

How long does it take?

The divorce itself has a built-in minimum of around 26 weeks — a 20-week period after applying, then a further 6-week period before the final order. Sorting out finances and children's arrangements can take longer, depending on how straightforward the situation is. We will give you a realistic picture from the start.

Can I afford a solicitor?

We offer a free initial consultation so you can understand your situation before committing to anything. We are upfront about costs from the start — we offer fixed-fee options where possible, and we will always tell you before we do anything that has a cost attached. You are in control.

Do I need my partner to agree before I can start?

No. You can apply for a divorce on your own. If both of you apply together, it can sometimes make the process smoother — but it is not required. We can advise you on the best approach for your situation.
