Worth noting
A publicly accessible internal document is indexed by Google. Your IT User Guide is reachable via a direct URL and appears in search results. This is a security and data governance issue that should be addressed immediately, regardless of any broader work.
Security
Your founding date is misrepresented on the live site. "Over 35 years" appears in at least one location. SRB was established in 1976: that's closer to 50 years. It's a trust signal worth correcting, and a story worth telling properly.
Brand
No meta descriptions on key service pages. Search engines are writing their own snippets, often pulling unhelpful text. Well-written meta descriptions are one of the lowest-effort, highest-return SEO improvements available.
SEO
Structured data (schema markup) is absent. Your offices, solicitors, services, and reviews aren't marked up in a way Google can read and feature. This directly affects your eligibility for rich results: the expanded listings that dominate local search.
SEO
Google Reviews aren't being actively cultivated. Satisfied clients aren't being invited to leave reviews in a consistent, structured way. For a firm of SRB's standing, this is an underused asset. Reviews are one of the most powerful local search signals available.
SEO
The site isn't optimised for mobile-first browsing. Google has indexed your site in mobile-first mode since 2021. Text size, tap targets, and page layout on smaller screens all fall short of current best practice and of the standards your clients deserve.
Accessibility
Page titles and H1 tags don't reflect how clients actually search. SRB is a strong brand, but someone searching for help with a divorce in Twickenham isn't searching for you by name. They're searching for the problem. There's a meaningful opportunity to capture that intent.
