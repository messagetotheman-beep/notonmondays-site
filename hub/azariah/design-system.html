Tone of Voice

Plain English. Short sentences. Active voice. We write for busy clinicians, commissioners and funders who do not have time for corporate filler.

Clear and direct
Say what you mean. Short sentences. Active voice. No unnecessary words between the reader and the point.
Example
"This platform helps clinicians stay compliant, faster."
Human and grounded
Write for people, not organisations. Acknowledge that compliance is hard work.
Example
"Compliance paperwork shouldn't take time away from patients."
Specific and evidenced
Make claims you can back up. Specific numbers, real outcomes, named NHS partners.
Example
"Reduced documentation time by 40% across three NHS trusts."
Optimistic but honest
Excited about improving NHS compliance. But no over-promising. Trust earned through honesty.
Example
"We're early stage. The problem is real, and the approach works."
Do / don't
Do write like this
Use short, active sentences
Name the specific benefit for the clinician
Use "we" and "you" — keep it personal
Back claims with numbers or examples
Use plain English for clinical jargon
Start with the outcome, not the process
Don't write like this
Don't use passive voice or corporate filler
Don't say "solution", "leverage" or "ecosystem"
Don't pad sentences to sound authoritative
Don't make claims you can't evidence
Don't use em dashes — use commas instead
Don't bury the point in a long preamble
