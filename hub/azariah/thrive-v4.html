Eligibility — confirm these before submitting
Legal structure
Lead must be a Research Institute, NHS provider, charity or CIC. SMEs cannot lead. Confirm Azariah's structure with Jok.
Technology readiness
Must be TRL 3 or above (experimental proof of concept as a minimum). State your TRL clearly in the form.
Technology type
Must be a Tier C digital health technology per the NICE evidence standards framework. Tier A and B are not eligible.
RMS account
Register now at nihr.ac.uk. Account validation can take up to two working days. Do not leave this until the last minute.
01 — Plain English Summary
What this project does
Public-facing · ~300 words · Scored heavily · Must be jargon-free and human

The NHS is built on care and trust. We are improving the systems that trust depends on.

NIHR guidance

This is the first thing reviewers read and is used on the NIHR website if funded. Write for a non-specialist: a clinician, a commissioner, or a member of the public. Answer four questions: what is the problem, who does it affect, what does Azariah do, and why does it matter now. Lead with care and trust.

02 — The Problem
Unmet need
~250 words · Frame as system failure, not people failure · Needs 2–3 real data points
NIHR guidance + what this needs

The deployment problem framing is solid. What is still missing: 2–3 specific data points from a named NHS source or published report. For example: average onboarding time, agency spend per trust, or percentage of unfilled bank shifts. Without these, the problem reads as assertion rather than evidence.

Key line: "The NHS does not just have a workforce shortage. It has a deployment problem. Capacity exists. The system cannot activate it fast enough."

03 — The Innovation
Compliance as infrastructure
~250 words · Strongest scoring section · Describe novelty clearly

Compliance should not be a barrier to care. It should be the infrastructure that enables it.

NIHR guidance

Describe what it is, how it works, and what is genuinely new about it. Be specific — reviewers need to understand the three components: the portable profile, the governance framework, and the audit trail. State clearly why this is different from existing approaches.

07 — Project Plan
How we will deliver this
~250 words · THRIVE is 9 months max · Three phases · Include success criteria
Important: duration correction

THRIVE funds up to £150,000 over 9 months — not 12–15 months as in the PDF draft. The project plan must be compressed. Compress Discovery to 2–3 months, Alpha to 3–4 months, Pilot to 2–3 months. The test question: can a clinician verified in Trust A be safely deployed in Trust B within 48–72 hours?

10 — Commercialisation and Scaling
Route beyond the project
~150 words · IP ownership must be addressed · Route to market needed
What is missing

NIHR specifically scores IP strategy and commercialisation pathway. You need to state: who owns the IP, what the intended route to market is (subscription, NHS procurement framework, spin-out, licence), and what the plan is if THRIVE funding ends. Even an early-stage answer is expected — vagueness on IP is a common reason applications are downgraded.

12 — Patient and Public Involvement (PPIE)
How patients shape this
~150 words · Mandatory · Must be a plan, not an intention · Scored heavily
What is missing

Applications with weak PPIE are regularly downgraded. You need to name: (1) the patient or public group you will engage, (2) the mechanism — patient panel, interviews, advisory group, or involvement in a specific NHS patient group, and (3) the exact project points where involvement will happen (discovery, prototype review, pilot feedback). The current draft states intent. This needs to be a plan.
