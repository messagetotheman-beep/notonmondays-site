Getting more value
from your CRM

Most organisations already have the system. The opportunity is to make it work harder for the business.

TL;DR

  • Most CRM challenges are operational, not just technical.
  • The biggest gains often come from improving how work flows across teams.
  • Better CRM alignment helps teams reduce manual effort, improve visibility, and make better decisions.
  • Salesforce, automation and Agentforce-style solutions work best when the operating model is clear.

What we keep seeing

Across growing organisations, the pattern is consistent.

A CRM exists. Often it is Salesforce. It is configured, it supports the business, and it plays an important role. But it is not yet delivering its full potential.

You will recognise this if:

  • Pipeline exists but conversion varies
  • Teams sometimes work outside the system
  • Data is captured but not consistently used
  • Reporting requires some manual effort
  • Automation helps in parts, but not end to end

Nothing is fundamentally broken. There is simply more value available than is currently being realised.

Where the opportunity sits

Most organisations improve their CRM by enhancing the system: adding fields, building dashboards, introducing automation, refining reports. These can help.

But the bigger opportunity often sits in how the organisation operates:

  • How work moves from sales into delivery
  • How teams share context across stages
  • How data is used day to day
  • How compliance and execution fit together

This is why organisations investing in CRM optimisation often see incremental gains rather than step changes. The system improves. But the way work flows stays the same.

Why CRM value gets stuck

When a CRM is close to working well but not fully aligned, friction is low-level but persistent:

  • Deals take slightly longer than they could
  • Teams spend more time coordinating than necessary
  • Data is reviewed before it is trusted
  • Opportunities to automate are missed

Individually, these are small. Together, they add up. Organisations can unlock 20 to 30% efficiency gains by improving operational flow. Teams often spend over half their time on coordination and admin. Better data quality significantly improves the speed of decisions.

Key point

Most CRM challenges are operational, not just technical. The system often works fine. The way work flows around it is where the opportunity sits.

What better alignment looks like

Small improvements to operational flow often create meaningful returns. The pattern is consistent across organisations at different stages of growth.

Before
  • Inconsistent data capture across teams
  • Manual handovers with gaps in context
  • Reports built from multiple sources
  • Automation that covers some cases, not all
The shift
  • Agree how work should flow before configuring the system
  • Define what data matters and where it lives
  • Build automation around how teams actually work
  • Make reporting something teams use to act, not just review
After
  • Data captured once and reused across the process
  • Clean handovers with shared understanding of scope
  • Reports that teams trust and act on
  • Automation that reflects how work actually moves

When this comes together, systems stop being a record of activity and start being a way to move work forward.

Where Salesforce fits

Salesforce is the CRM we work with most often. When the operating model is clear and data flows cleanly, it becomes a real operational asset.

When it is not, it can create the appearance of progress without the substance. Data sits in the system. Reports exist. But decisions still happen outside it.

Getting more from Salesforce is usually a question of alignment, not configuration. The platform has the capability. The question is whether the organisation is set up to use it.

What this means for teams

As organisations grow, the cost of misalignment increases. More clients, more complexity, more reliance on systems. At the same time, expectations rise: faster response times, better visibility, more consistent outcomes.

Teams that have aligned their CRM with how work actually flows tend to:

  • Spend less time on coordination and admin
  • Make better use of the data they already have
  • Onboard clients and customers more smoothly
  • Trust their reporting enough to act on it

The result is not just a better system. It is a better way of working.
