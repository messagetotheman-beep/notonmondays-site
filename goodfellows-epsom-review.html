A quick note before you're fully open

Congratulations on the new opening. Getting a firm off the ground takes real effort, and the operational side rarely gets the attention it deserves at the start.

This is a short, honest review of what a potential client sees when they look you up online today — and a few simple things worth fixing early. It's not a proposal. It's just useful information.

What a potential client sees right now

If someone searches for Goodfellows Epsom and finds your website, this is what they get:

Index of /
A plain directory listing. No content, no branding, no information about the firm.

In plain terms: the website exists, but nothing has been published to it yet. To most people, that looks like something is broken — or that the firm isn't quite ready.

Specific gaps right now:

  • No firm name, logo, or basic description visible
  • No list of services or areas of law
  • No contact details, address, or phone number
  • No indication the firm is open and taking clients
  • Nothing to build trust or credibility at first glance

Why this matters now

Most people choosing a solicitor in a new area do the same thing: they search, they look at a few options, and they make a quick decision.

That decision often happens within 30 seconds. If the website is unclear, broken, or missing basic information — they move on. They don't call to check. They don't come back later.

This is especially true for people who don't already know you. Your existing network will find you regardless. But anyone new in Epsom searching for legal help won't wait.

What good looks like (at this stage)

You don't need anything complicated right now. A straightforward, working website with the right basics in place is enough to make the right first impression.

  • Clear services — what areas of law you cover, in plain language
  • Immediate trust — firm name, who you are, SRA registration visible
  • Simple contact — phone number, email, address — easy to find
  • Mobile-friendly — most people will arrive on a phone
  • Fast and accessible — loads quickly, readable for everyone
  • Local relevance — mentions Epsom, serves the area, feels local

What I would focus on first

In rough order of priority:

  • Get something live quickly — even a single, well-structured page is far better than what's there now
  • Structure content for clarity — services, who you are, how to get in touch
  • Simple enquiry flow — a contact form or clear call-to-action so people can take the next step easily
  • Lay the right foundations — done properly from the start, this also sets you up well for search visibility and future systems
