Digital First Impressions Review

A short review of what a potential client sees when they look you up online today, and a few simple things worth addressing early. This is not a proposal. It is just useful information.

Prepared by Not On Mondays  ·  April 2026  ·  Informal and without obligation

Section 1

What a potential client sees right now

If someone searches for Goodfellows Epsom and clicks through to the website, here is what they find:

Website — as seen today

Index of /

A plain directory listing. No firm name, no content, no way to find out who you are or how to get in touch.

In plain terms: the web server is live, but nothing has been published to it yet. To most visitors this looks broken, or as though the firm is not yet open.

  • No firm name, logo, or description visible
  • No services listed or areas of law explained
  • No phone number, email address, or office location
  • No signal that the firm is open and accepting clients
  • Nothing that builds trust or credibility at first glance

Section 2

Why this matters

Most people choosing a solicitor in a new area follow the same pattern. They search, they look at two or three options, and they decide quickly.

01

The decision often happens in under 30 seconds. If the site is unclear or broken, people move on without calling to check.

02

Your existing network will find you regardless. But anyone new to Epsom searching for legal help will not wait.

03

A weak digital presence does not just cost first impressions. Over time it affects how the firm appears in local search results.

Section 3

What good looks like at this stage

You do not need anything complicated right now. A straightforward, working website with the right basics in place is enough.

  • Clear services. What areas of law you cover, in plain language.
  • Immediate trust. Firm name, who you are, SRA registration visible.
  • Simple contact. Phone number, email, and address easy to find.
  • Mobile-friendly. Most visitors will arrive on a phone.
  • Fast and accessible. Loads quickly, readable for everyone.
  • Local relevance. Mentions Epsom, serves the area, feels grounded.

Section 4

Recommended first steps

In rough order of priority:

  1. Get something live quickly. A single, well-structured page is far better than what is there now. It does not need to be perfect at launch.
  2. Structure content for clarity. Services, who you are, and how to get in touch. Written in plain English, not legal language.
  3. Add a simple enquiry flow. A contact form or clear call to action so people can take the next step without having to search for a phone number.
  4. Lay the right foundations. Done properly from the start, this also sets you up for search visibility, future client systems, and growth over time.
