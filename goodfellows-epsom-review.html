Section 1

What a potential client sees right now

If someone searches for Goodfellows Epsom and clicks through, here is what they find:

Website — as seen today

Index of /

A plain directory listing. No firm name, no content, no way to make contact.

The server is live, but nothing has been published. To most visitors, this looks broken or as though the firm is not yet open.

  • No firm name, logo, or description visible
  • No services or areas of law listed
  • No phone number, email, or address
  • No indication the firm is open
  • Nothing that establishes trust

Section 2

Why this matters

People choosing a solicitor search, compare two or three options, and decide quickly.

01

Under 30 seconds. If the site is unclear or broken, they won't call to check. They'll move on to the next firm.

02

Someone searching "solicitor near me Epsom" will open a few firms side by side and choose the one that feels clearest and easiest to contact.

03

A missing or broken site also affects how the firm ranks in local search over time.

Section 3

What good looks like at this stage

You do not need anything complicated. A clean, working website with the basics in place is enough.

  • Clear services. What areas of law you cover, in plain language.
  • Immediate trust. Firm name, who you are, SRA registration visible.
  • Simple contact. Phone number, email, and address easy to find.
  • Mobile-friendly. Most visitors will arrive on a phone.
  • Fast and accessible. Loads quickly, works for everyone.
  • Local. Mentions Epsom, serves the community, feels rooted here.

Section 4

Recommended first steps

  1. Get something live. A single, well-structured page is far better than what is there now. It does not need to be perfect.
  2. Structure content clearly. Services, who you are, how to get in touch. Plain English throughout.
  3. Add a simple enquiry flow. A contact form so people can reach you without hunting for a number.
  4. Lay the right foundations. Done well now, this sets you up for local search visibility and future client systems.
